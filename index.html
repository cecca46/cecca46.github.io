Francesco Ceccarelli

I am a PhD student at the University of Cambridge with research interests in machine learning, computational biology, graph representation learning, and multimodal scientific data analysis.

My work broadly explores how computational methods can learn from structured, heterogeneous, and biologically meaningful data, with applications across protein modelling, spatial biology, and disease-related biomedical research.

Research interests

Machine learning for structured data

I am interested in models that can reason over relational, graph-structured, and non-Euclidean data in ways that go beyond standard learning pipelines.

Computational biology

I work on computational approaches for biological problems, especially where structure, sequence, or spatial context are central to the scientific question.

Multimodal and spatial data integration

A recurring theme in my work is integrating heterogeneous data sources, including molecular, spatial, and phenotypic information.

About me
